# ROB-348: Mentor Assignment for Competition Teams

## Overview
This document outlines the mentor requirements, identification process, training plan, and assignment strategy for RoboKids Vietnam's VEX and FLL competition teams.

---

## Mentor Requirements

### Head Mentor (1 per team)

#### Technical Skills
- [ ] Strong understanding of robotics mechanics (gears, motors, sensors)
- [ ] Programming experience (Blockly, Python, or C++)
- [ ] Familiarity with VEX/FLL competition rules and formats
- [ ] Troubleshooting and debugging skills
- [ ] Experience with competition robot design

#### Coaching Skills
- [ ] Patient and encouraging teaching style
- [ ] Ability to manage a team of 4-10 students
- [ ] Time management (practice scheduling, competition prep)
- [ ] Communication with parents
- [ ] Conflict resolution among team members

#### Availability
- [ ] 4-6 hours per week during competition season (June-December)
- [ ] Full availability during competition days
- [ ] Attendance at weekly team practices
- [ ] Willingness to travel to local/regional events

### Assistant Mentor (1 per team)

#### Technical Skills
- [ ] Basic robotics knowledge
- [ ] Willingness to learn advanced concepts
- [ ] Hands-on building experience
- [ ] Basic programming understanding

#### Support Skills
- [ ] Administrative support (scheduling, documentation)
- [ ] Student supervision during practices
- [ ] Equipment management
- [ ] Parent communication support

## Mentor Training Plan

### Week 1: Orientation
- [ ] Company and program overview
- [ ] RoboKids teaching methodology
- [ ] Student age groups and learning styles
- [ ] Competition team structure and goals

### Week 2: Competition Rules
- [ ] VEX 2026 game rules and scoring
- [ ] FLL 2026 challenge breakdown
- [ ] Inspection requirements
- [ ] Competition day procedures

### Week 3: Technical Training (VEX)
- [ ] VEX EDR/SPOKE kit components
- [ ] Robot building techniques
- [ ] VEXcode programming interface
- [ ] Sensor integration

### Week 4: Technical Training (FLL)
- [ ] LEGO Spike Prime/EV3 components
- [ ] FLL robot game strategies
- [ ] LEGO Education SPIKE programming
- [ ] Project and Core Values facilitation

### Week 5: Coaching Methods
- [ ] Team management and motivation
- [ ] Practice session planning
- [ ] Student assessment techniques
- [ ] Parent communication best practices

### Week 6: Certification
- [ ] VEX Robotics Competition Coach Certification (free, online)
- [ ] FIRST LEGO League Coach Certification (free, online)
- [ ] RoboKids Competition Mentor Handbook issued
- [ ] Mentorship agreement signed

## Schedule & Commitment Agreements

### Commitment Period
**Competition Season: June - December 2026 (7 months)**

### Weekly Time Commitment
| Activity | Head Mentor | Assistant Mentor |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Team Practice (2 hrs) | Required | Required |
| Prep/Planning (1 hr) | Required | Optional |
| Competition Days | Required | Required |
| Communication/Admin (1 hr) | Required | Optional |
| **Total per Week** | **4-5 hours** | **2-3 hours** |

### Practice Schedule
- **Day:** Saturday
- **Time:** 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM (VEX), 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM (FLL)
- **Location:** RoboKids HQ or partner school
- **Break:** No practice during Vietnamese holidays

